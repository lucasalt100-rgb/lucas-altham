Experience

PwC New World New Skills Work Experience

A 4-day work experience programme which allowed me to gain insight into the firm and what professional services they offer. Working and learning with like-minded peers and members of PwC was incredibly useful as I got to bring my ideas to life and work as a team to complete activities and tasks together. This experience sharpened many of my soft skills however to spotlight one in particular I would pick out public speaking (presenting). Me and my team had to present to a PwC panel and propose solutions to a fashion business wanting to 'save the high street' and operate more in their physical stores. I had to get the key messages across to the panel and I did this by maintaining eye contact and focusing on what matters the most for the business. Having confidence in our ideas was also necessary as after we got questioned in relation to our solutions. We got given feedback on how to improve which was invaluable and I will definitely consider it in future presentations.

Data Management Course

An online session run by QA Ltd which developed my knowledge of data and its uses in computing. The main part of the session was to introduce me to SQL and start to understand how it works. I learnt all the commands and then tackled some complex problems involving SQL. We were then split into teams and had to complete one final task which incorporated everything we had learnt in that session. I had to use effective communication to express my thoughts over the call and work efficiently as a team.

Grant Thornton Insight Day

I was lucky enough to attend a Grant Thornton Insight Evening at one of their London offices and was astounded by the vast range of professional services they offer! I spoke to a variety of members at GT and they informed me about what they do in their job, this allowed me to focus on specific areas I was more interested in for the future. Learning more about others who are in a place I inspire to be in was nothing short of memorable, as I also had deep conversation with current GT employees as to how they go to where they are now. Listening to others talk about their journey really made me realise the importance of networking and to keep on going no matter what.

Student Mentoring

Throughout Year 12 I have taken part in the 'Guardians of Learning' mentoring programme. The programme involves supporting students in lower years and helping them flourish with their studies. I was given a maths class in year 8 where once a week I came to their maths lesson and assisted the students through that lesson. When students asked for help with maths questions I had to attempt to break it down as simply and clearly as possible so the students would be able to understand without any issues. Not only did I have to help with problem solving, I found myself having to keep the students motivated and making sure they paid attention. To combat the distractions I had to try to be as engaging as possible so for example I sometimes used real life examples that can relate to some maths problems they were doing and also I added breaks so the students did not get too overloaded with the work.
